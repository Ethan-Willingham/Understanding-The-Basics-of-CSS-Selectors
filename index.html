Interesting Facts about Kitten Development

"p" element immediatly following an "h2" element

  • The largest number of kittens born to a cat in a single litter is 19. This record was set by a four-year-old Burmese/Siamese cat named Tarawood Antigone in 1970 in the UK. Out of these, 15 survived.
  • Most cats have 18 toes, with five on their front paws and four on the back. However, some cats, known as polydactyl cats, can have as many as 28 toes in total! The Guinness World Record for the cat with the most toes is 28, shared by several polydactyl cats.
  • Himmy, a desexed tabby cat from Queensland, Australia, weighed in at a staggering 21.3 kg (46.8 lbs) in 1986. This made Himmy the heaviest cat ever recorded. Since then, the Guinness World Records has stopped recording "heaviest pets" to discourage overfeeding.
  • It's possible for a single litter of kittens to have multiple fathers. This phenomenon is known as superfecundation. If a female cat mates with more than one male cat while she's in heat, different kittens in the resulting litter can have different fathers.
